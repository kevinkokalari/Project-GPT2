Poor soul, the center of my sinful earth,
Pressed with these rebel powers that thee array,
Why dost thou pine within and suffer dearth,
Struck in thy own thoughts, with others,
And love do I not love thy father,
Where, on the eye, thou tellst me of thy mother's condition,
Or of her future prosperity?
Seek thy mother's name, thou wilt find
And make me like my father's worth;
In thy grief, thou shalt surely give me hope of the future.
  But I have heard of thy goodness, dost thou not love thy father's pity?

Love is too young to know what conscience is;
Yet who knows not conscience is born of love?
Then, gentle cheater, urge not my amiss,
My heart was so far reared
And played up so high a stage.
I found my voice grow bolder and bolder;

7
In loving thee thou know'st I am forsworn,
But thou art twice forsworn, to me love swearing;
In act thy bed-vow broke, and new faith torn;
Bridging up thy chalice, thou canst stand thy breath,
And as yet, after thy fall, I watch thee as fornication.
A plague of jest thou wilt keep,
And oft thy chalice, being struck, I find drown;
From over thy chalice, thy teeth gape;
Thy tears hide thy tongue, my brother wretch.
  Alas, wilt thou, before thy end, forbear,
  Or by it, wilt thou, too, the chalice.

Cupid laid by his brand and fell asleep.
A maid of Dian's this advantage found,
And his love-kindling fire did quickly steep
Where each hot and hot day had often before torn
And broken the joy of her love in the tempest.
Whilst all was at an end, he slept with joy,
Nor saw and looked on the day that morning,
For when his alarm had not been told,
His fortune was lost; but I, in a little time,
Would grant him full satisfaction;
Yet this, still more, I thought of more dear to him
Than his dear dost, or his cruel slander
Which can live to this end.
  But it was in my worthiness that he should be taken care
  When, on my part, his love was much vexed.
  As if to weigh him out, he would well like
  To live my happiness; and then, in a short time,
  He should weep that he had heard that he had loved nothing.
